Thesis: Temperature Response of Photosynthesis in Betula pendula Provenances from Southern and Northern Finland.
Geographic provenances of tree species with wide geographical distribution represent an important source of phenotypic and genotypic variation structured along environmental clines. Such local adaptations are expected to hinder trees capacity to respond to climate change. Photosynthesis as a key biological process is very sensitive to changes in the environment. Temperature response of photosynthesis is characterized by a parabolic curve with maximum rate of Photosynthesis reached at optimum temperature. The thesis concentrates on the temperature response of photosynthesis in two provenances of silver birch (B. pendula) from northern (Kittil√§) and southern (Punkaharju) Finland.

Mikkeli University of Applied Sciences (2013 -2016)

Thesis: Wood Preservation: Kiln Drying.
Wood preservation refers to the measures taken to ensure life time of wood products, mostly timber. They typically include chemical impregnations and coatings that prevent microbial and insect activity. However, prior to chemical treatment and other further uses of timber, it needs to be dried. Timber drying is done in drying kilns. Energy efficiency of traditional kilns is low because of the need of air exchange with the environment. Dehumidification kilns use a closed loop system where water vapor is condensed by air cooling which is then heated and returned. Cooling and heating occur in dehumidifier unit, which represents a refrigerator with heater and condenser coils. The thesis describes the installation and testing of such kiln in a small-scale sawmill.
